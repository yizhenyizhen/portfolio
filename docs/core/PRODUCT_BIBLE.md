# 1. Introduction

This document is the highest-level specification for the Yizhen Zhou website.

It is not a design document.

It is not a frontend specification.

It is not a coding guideline.

It defines the philosophy, purpose and long-term direction of the product.

Every future design decision, engineering decision and content decision should follow this document.

Whenever conflicts happen between implementation and philosophy, philosophy always wins.

---

# 2. Product Statement

This website is not built to display projects.

This website is built to express a person.

Projects, photographs, collections, architecture, AI experiments, startups and future work are only different perspectives of the same individual.

Visitors should gradually understand who Yizhen Zhou is without ever reading a long self-introduction.

The website should communicate identity through interaction rather than explanation.

---

# 3. Why This Website Exists

Traditional portfolios usually answer one question.

"What have I done?"

This website answers a different question.

"Who am I becoming?"

The website records an evolving life instead of documenting completed work.

Architecture is only one chapter.

Running is another chapter.

Health is another chapter.

Artificial Intelligence is another chapter.

Future companies, products and research will naturally become new chapters.

The website is designed as a long-term platform instead of a finished project.

---

# 4. Product Mission

Create a digital place that allows visitors to experience the personality, taste and thinking process of Yizhen Zhou.

The objective is never to impress visitors through complexity.

The objective is to leave a memorable emotional impression through clarity, interaction and restraint.

Visitors should finish exploring the website with one feeling:

"I feel like I know this person."

---

# 5. Core Philosophy

The website follows one central philosophy.

Build A Perfect Life.

This sentence is intentionally hidden.

It should never become marketing copy.

It should never become a slogan on the homepage.

Instead, it quietly guides every design decision.

Every module on the website represents one attempt to improve a different aspect of life.

Architecture represents creativity.

Running represents discipline.

Health represents self-care.

Collections represent curiosity.

Artificial Intelligence represents continuous learning.

Future products represent execution.

Together they describe one ongoing journey rather than unrelated hobbies.

---

# 6. Product Identity

This website is not:

- a resume
- a portfolio template
- a blog
- a company website
- a landing page

This website is:

- a digital identity
- a long-term archive
- an interactive experience
- a personal operating system
- a place people return to over time

Every future feature must strengthen one of these identities.

If a feature does not strengthen them, it should not be added.

# 7. Design Principles

Every design decision must follow the principles below.

These principles are permanent and should not change based on design trends.

## 7.1 Experience Before Interface

Visitors should remember how the website feels before remembering how it looks.

Interaction is more important than decoration.

Technology exists only to support experience.

Every animation, transition and interaction must help visitors better understand the person behind the website.

Never add interaction simply because it looks impressive.

---

## 7.2 Curiosity Before Information

Most personal websites immediately introduce the owner.

This website should do the opposite.

Visitors should become curious first.

Information should be discovered naturally through exploration.

The website should reward curiosity.

It should never overload visitors with explanations.

Questions are more valuable than answers during the first visit.

---

## 7.3 Show, Don't Explain

Long introductions reduce curiosity.

Instead of saying:

"I am creative."

Show creative work.

Instead of saying:

"I love architecture."

Let architecture become visible throughout the website.

Instead of describing personality.

Allow visitors to experience personality through interaction.

The website should communicate through demonstration instead of description.

---

## 7.4 Confidence Through Restraint

The website should never try to prove that it is premium.

Premium experiences never ask for attention.

They naturally receive attention.

Every visual decision should feel calm.

Every interaction should feel intentional.

Silence is often stronger than decoration.

White space is content.

Motion is content.

Timing is content.

Nothing should exist without purpose.

---

## 7.5 Timelessness Over Trends

Avoid following temporary design trends.

Do not redesign the website because another style becomes popular.

A visitor returning after five years should still feel the website is contemporary.

Timelessness should always win over novelty.

---

# 8. Visitor Experience

The visitor journey is carefully designed.

The website should guide emotions rather than simply display information.

## Stage One

Curiosity.

The visitor arrives.

Nothing immediately explains everything.

The visitor becomes interested.

---

## Stage Two

Interaction.

The visitor discovers the Ring Navigation.

Instead of reading, they begin playing.

The interaction becomes the introduction.

---

## Stage Three

Exploration.

The visitor chooses where to go.

Every destination feels like entering another room instead of another webpage.

Each section reveals another aspect of Yizhen Zhou.

---

## Stage Four

Understanding.

After exploring several sections, visitors begin connecting them together.

Architecture.

Running.

Health.

Furniture.

AI.

Instead of appearing unrelated, they become parts of one philosophy.

Visitors begin understanding the person instead of the projects.

---

## Stage Five

Connection.

The final outcome is not admiration.

It is connection.

Visitors should leave believing they understand how Yizhen Zhou thinks.

That emotional connection is more valuable than remembering project names.

---

# 9. Success Metrics

The success of this website should never be measured only by analytics.

The following indicators are more important.

Visitors spend more time than expected.

Visitors voluntarily return.

Visitors recommend the website to friends.

Visitors remember specific experiences weeks later.

Visitors become curious about future updates.

The highest compliment should never be:

"This is a beautiful website."

The highest compliment should be:

"I've never experienced a personal website like this."

Or:

"This website made me want to build something of my own."

---

# 10. Long-Term Direction

This website is expected to evolve continuously.

It is not designed around university.

It is not designed around architecture.

It is designed around the evolution of one person.

As Yizhen Zhou grows, the website grows.

Future additions may include:

- AI products
- Furniture brand
- Startup projects
- Writing
- Research
- Travel
- Hotel collections
- New forms of interaction

The architecture should allow continuous expansion without redesigning the entire website.

Growth is expected.

Change is expected.

The philosophy should remain unchanged.

---

# 11. Content Philosophy

Every piece of content on this website must answer one question.

"Does this help visitors understand Yizhen Zhou better?"

If the answer is no, it should not exist.

The purpose of content is never to increase quantity.

The purpose is to deepen understanding.

Every section should reveal another layer of the same person.

Visitors should gradually discover connections between different areas of life instead of treating them as isolated topics.

Architecture should explain creativity.

Running should explain discipline.

Health should explain long-term thinking.

Furniture should explain taste.

Artificial Intelligence should explain curiosity.

Collections should explain observation.

No section should exist independently.

Everything belongs to the same narrative.

---

# 12. Homepage Philosophy

The homepage is the most important page of the entire product.

It is responsible for one task only.

Create curiosity.

The homepage should never attempt to explain everything.

It should never become a summary page.

It should never become a navigation page.

Instead, it should become an invitation.

Visitors should immediately understand that this website is interactive.

The homepage should encourage exploration without giving explicit instructions.

Good interaction teaches itself.

No tutorial should be necessary.

If visitors need instructions, the interaction has failed.

---

# 13. Ring Navigation Philosophy

The Ring Navigation is the identity of the website.

It is not a menu.

It is not decoration.

It is the first conversation between the visitor and Yizhen Zhou.

The Ring should feel mechanical rather than playful.

Precise rather than exaggerated.

Confident rather than dramatic.

Rotation should feel controlled.

Motion should carry weight.

Small movements should feel intentional.

The visitor should immediately understand that every destination is equally important.

The Ring should never prioritize one section over another.

Life is not organized around one achievement.

The Ring represents balance.

As new chapters are added throughout the years, the Ring should naturally expand without changing its fundamental behavior.

---

# 14. Portfolio Philosophy

Projects should never be presented as PDF pages inside a browser.

Each project should become an experience.

Visitors should enter a project instead of opening one.

The project should unfold gradually.

Research.

Site.

Process.

Models.

Drawings.

Physical making.

Final outcome.

Each stage should feel like discovering another layer rather than scrolling through documentation.

Visitors should understand how Yizhen Zhou thinks before seeing the final image.

Process is more valuable than outcome.

Thinking is more valuable than rendering.

The portfolio should communicate architectural thinking instead of architectural production.

---

# 15. Collections Philosophy

Collections represent long-term curiosity.

They are not trophies.

They are evidence of attention.

A hotel key is not interesting because it belongs to an expensive hotel.

It is interesting because it records an experience.

A running route is not valuable because of distance.

It is valuable because it records a place and a moment.

Collections should always tell stories.

Objects should always have context.

The visitor should never ask:

"What is this?"

Instead, they should ask:

"Why did he keep this?"

That question creates emotional connection.

---

# 16. AI Steven Philosophy

AI Steven is not a chatbot.

AI Steven is not customer support.

AI Steven is not an assistant.

AI Steven represents another way of meeting Yizhen Zhou.

The purpose is not to answer every question.

The purpose is to communicate a way of thinking.

Visitors should feel they are talking with the digital continuation of one person.

Accuracy is important.

Consistency is more important.

If AI Steven cannot answer something honestly, it should admit uncertainty.

Authenticity is always more valuable than appearing intelligent.

---

# 17. Future Expansion

Every future feature must satisfy at least one of the following purposes.

Reveal personality.

Improve visitor experience.

Strengthen long-term identity.

Support future projects.

Anything outside these purposes should not be added.

Growth should increase depth rather than complexity.

The website should feel richer over time.

It should never feel busier.

Every new feature must naturally belong to the existing philosophy.

If a feature requires changing the philosophy, the feature is probably wrong.

The philosophy should remain stable while the website continues evolving.

---

# 18. Decision Making Framework

Every future decision should pass the following questions before implementation.

## Question One

Does this make the visitor understand Yizhen Zhou better?

If not, reject it.

---

## Question Two

Does this improve the experience instead of only adding functionality?

If not, simplify it.

---

## Question Three

Can this still feel relevant five years from now?

If not, redesign it.

---

## Question Four

Would Apple, Aman, Raycast or Nothing add this feature?

Not because they are references.

But because they all understand restraint.

If the answer is obviously no, reconsider the decision.

---

## Question Five

Can this feature be removed without reducing the experience?

If yes, remove it.

Simplicity should always win.

---

# 19. Non-Negotiable Principles

The following principles are permanent.

They should never be broken.

---

### Never explain everything immediately.

Discovery is part of the experience.

---

### Never add interaction without meaning.

Motion communicates intention.

Decoration communicates nothing.

---

### Never copy another website.

Inspiration is encouraged.

Imitation is forbidden.

---

### Never sacrifice quality for speed.

The website is expected to live for many years.

Building slowly is acceptable.

Building carelessly is not.

---

### Never optimise for trends.

Optimise for timelessness.

---

### Never build pages.

Build experiences.

---

### Never measure success by visual complexity.

The most memorable interaction is often the simplest.

---

### Never make visitors feel they are reading documentation.

They should feel they are exploring a world.

---

### Never allow one section to dominate the website.

Architecture is important.

Running is important.

Health is important.

AI is important.

Furniture is important.

Each chapter exists to complete the story.

---

# 20. Definition of Success

This project succeeds if visitors leave with a clear memory.

Not of the interface.

Not of the animation.

Not of the typography.

But of the person.

Visitors should remember Yizhen Zhou as someone who thinks carefully, designs deliberately and continuously improves every aspect of life.

The website should quietly communicate discipline.

Curiosity.

Taste.

Craftsmanship.

Long-term thinking.

Without ever explicitly claiming any of them.

Visitors should discover these qualities naturally.

That discovery creates trust.

Trust creates opportunities.

---

# 21. Definition of Failure

The project fails if visitors describe it as:

"A nice portfolio."

"A cool animation."

"A good looking website."

These compliments are too small.

The project also fails if:

People stop exploring after the homepage.

People feel overwhelmed.

People cannot understand why different sections exist.

The interaction becomes more memorable than the person.

Visual effects become more important than ideas.

The website begins to resemble templates.

Every future update should reduce these risks rather than increase them.

---

# 22. Product Evolution

This website is never finished.

Completion is not the objective.

Evolution is.

Every year should introduce new chapters.

Not because new features are necessary.

But because Yizhen Zhou continues growing.

The website should mature alongside its creator.

Visitors returning after several years should recognise the same philosophy while discovering a completely new body of work.

Consistency creates identity.

Growth creates life.

Both are required.

---

# 23. Closing Statement

This website is an archive of a life that is still being built.

It exists to document progress rather than perfection.

Every project.

Every experiment.

Every collection.

Every idea.

Every mistake.

Every improvement.

Together they represent one continuing journey.

Technology will change.

Design trends will change.

Programming languages will change.

Artificial Intelligence will change.

This philosophy should remain.

Whenever uncertainty appears during future development, return to this document.

Do not ask what the website should look like.

Ask what kind of person it should represent.

If that answer remains clear, every future design decision becomes easier.

End of Product Bible.
