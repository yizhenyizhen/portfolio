Version: 1.0 (Draft)
Project: Yizhen Zhou
Status: In Progress

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines how visitors explore the digital identity of Yizhen Zhou.

It is not a sitemap.

It is not a navigation document.

It defines the journey through which visitors gradually understand one person.

The architecture is designed around actions instead of categories.

Visitors should never feel they are browsing pages.

They should feel they are discovering a life.

---

# 2. Information Philosophy

Traditional websites organise information.

This website organises curiosity.

Visitors should not immediately understand everything.

Instead, every interaction should naturally lead to another question.

Curiosity creates exploration.

Exploration creates understanding.

Understanding creates trust.

Trust becomes the purpose of the entire website.

---

# 3. Experience Structure

Every visitor follows the same emotional journey.

Arrive.

↓

Become curious.

↓

Interact.

↓

Explore.

↓

Understand.

↓

Remember.

Different visitors may choose different paths.

The destination remains the same.

Understanding Yizhen Zhou.

---

# 4. Ring Navigation

The Ring is not navigation.

The Ring is the beginning of exploration.

Every item inside the Ring represents an action.

Not a destination.

Actions invite participation.

Categories invite reading.

This project always prefers participation.

The Ring should always remain minimal.

It should never become crowded.

It should never become a list of pages.

The Ring is permanent.

Its contents may evolve.

Its philosophy never changes.

---

# 5. The Five Actions

The website consists of five permanent actions.

Create

Collect

Discover

Build

Meet

These actions define how visitors explore the website.

Every future feature should naturally belong to one of them.

If a feature cannot fit into these actions, reconsider the feature before implementing it.

---

# 6. Create

Purpose

Show how ideas become reality.

This world represents creation.

Current content includes:

Architecture

Portfolio

Design Process

Models

Drawings

Future content may include:

Furniture Design

Industrial Design

Competitions

Creative Research

Visitors should leave this world understanding how Yizhen Zhou creates.

Not simply what he has created.

---

# 7. Collect

Purpose

Reveal long-term curiosity.

Collections are memories.

Not trophies.

Objects.

Places.

Experiences.

Everything collected should explain something about the person behind it.

Possible collections include:

Room Keys

Running Routes

Health

Photography

Music

Books

Travel Memories

Future collections should naturally expand here.

# 8. Discover

Purpose

Expand perspective.

Discovery is not about collecting information.

It is about discovering new ways of seeing the world.

This world records places, experiences and ideas that have shaped Yizhen Zhou.

Current content may include:

Hotels

Travel

Cities

Architecture Visits

Notes

Future Inspirations

Unlike Collections, which preserve memories, Discover represents continuous exploration.

Visitors should leave this world understanding what inspires Yizhen Zhou.

Not where he has been.

---

# 9. Build

Purpose

Show ideas becoming systems.

Build represents execution.

This world is dedicated to projects that extend beyond personal interests.

Current content may include:

Artificial Intelligence

Horizon

Furniture Brand

Trading Research

Future Products

Future Companies

Every project inside Build should answer one question.

"What is Yizhen Zhou trying to create for the future?"

This world should constantly evolve.

Some projects may disappear.

Some may become independent companies.

The website should always preserve their origins.

---

# 10. Meet

Purpose

Create connection.

This is the final destination of the visitor journey.

By the time visitors arrive here, they should already understand who Yizhen Zhou is.

This page should not introduce the person.

It should simply make conversation possible.

Current content may include:

AI Steven

Resume

Contact

Instagram

LinkedIn

Email

Future collaborations begin here.

Visitors should feel they are meeting someone they already know.

---

# 11. Information Flow

The architecture follows a progressive disclosure model.

Information should never appear all at once.

Every interaction reveals only the next layer.

Homepage

↓

Action

↓

World

↓

Story

↓

Details

↓

Conversation

Visitors should always feel there is another layer waiting to be explored.

Curiosity should continuously pull the experience forward.

---

# 12. Navigation Principles

Navigation should disappear.

Visitors should rarely think about moving between pages.

Transitions should feel like walking through connected spaces.

Every destination should remain visually connected to the homepage.

The Ring is the beginning of every journey.

The visitor may leave it.

But its design language should continue throughout the website.

Every world should feel familiar.

Every world should also feel unique.

---

# 13. Scalability

This architecture is designed to evolve for at least ten years.

Future additions should not create new first-level actions.

Instead, they should naturally belong to one existing world.

Examples.

A future photography collection belongs to Collect.

A future architecture competition belongs to Create.

A future startup belongs to Build.

A future book recommendation belongs to Discover.

A future personal assistant belongs to Meet.

Growth should increase depth.

Never increase complexity.

---

# 14. Visitor Memory

Visitors should not remember the structure of the website.

They should remember the journey.

If asked one week later what they experienced, they should not answer:

"I visited five pages."

Instead, they should answer:

"I explored someone's life."

The architecture succeeds when navigation becomes invisible.

---

# 15. Closing Statement

Information Architecture is invisible when designed correctly.

Visitors should never notice how information is organised.

They should only feel that every next step is natural.

This architecture is not built around content.

It is built around curiosity.

Every future feature should strengthen this feeling.

Whenever uncertainty appears, ask one question.

"Does this make exploration more natural?"

If the answer is no, redesign the architecture before writing code.

End of Information Architecture.
